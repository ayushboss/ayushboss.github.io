Linguistics is the study and analysis of languages and the cultures they govern. I've had a lot of experience field from a direct perspective of simply learning languages, but I plan to do some more work into closer syntactical analysis of linguistic construction (NLP is something I want to look into).

  • English (native, fluent)
  • Tamil (native, proficient)
    • my mother tongue, as both sides of my family are from the state of Tamil Nadu in India
  • Bosnian/Croatian/Serbian (proficient)
    • picked up the dialects of Serbo-Croatian during my time in Bosnia as a Kennedy-Lugar YES Abroad Scholar in 2019 and 2020
  • Latin
    • active member of the Houston Latin community since 2014
    • works I've read and analyzed include
      • Caesar's De Bello Gallico
      • Ovid's The Metamorphoses
      • Vergil's The Aeneid
      • Livy's Ab Urbe Condita
      • Cicero's Ad Catalinam and De Imperio Cn. Pompei

I'm definitely looking to expand my horizons a lot in this field. I plan to look into these next.

  • Conlangs
    • Conlangs (constructed languages) are languages built by humans as opposed to naturally developed often used to bridge communication between speakers of different languages or simply to emphasize the art of language. I'm currently learning Esperanto, the most notable conlang, and I want to develop my own conlangs in the near future.
  • Natural Language Processing
    • Combining my math, computer science, and linguistic backgrounds, I'm excited to see how NLP can be applied not only to facilitate modern applications but also how it can be used as a means of preserving minority languages.
